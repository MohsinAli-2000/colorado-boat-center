Finding the Right Boat Loan

Boat financing works a little differently than auto loans. Because boats are considered recreational purchases, interest rates may be higher, but loan terms are often longer—helping keep monthly payments more affordable. Titling and tax requirements can also vary by state, which is why working with an experienced dealer and lender can make the process much smoother.

Most boat loans fall into two categories:

Secured Loans

The most common option, where the boat itself serves as collateral.

Unsecured Loans

Less common and typically carry higher interest rates because there is no collateral.

Protect Your Investment with Boat Insurance

While boat insurance is not required in Colorado, Colorado Boat Center strongly recommends coverage to protect your investment. Insurance can help cover damages, liability, and other unexpected events so you can enjoy boating with peace of mind.

Colorado Boat Center can connect you with a trusted marine insurance provider that specializes exclusively in boats and marine products, helping you find the right coverage at competitive rates.

What Marine Insurance Typically Includes

Boating Liability

Protection if you are responsible for injury or property damage to another party

Uninsured Boater Protection

Covers damages if you are involved in an accident with an uninsured boater

Physical Damage

Covers damage from accidents, storms, fire, theft, or vandalism

Towing Assistance

Helps cover costs if your boat breaks down on the water

Equipment Coverage

Protects items such as fishing gear, tow equipment, and electronics

Fuel Spill & Wreckage Cleanup

Covers environmental cleanup and removal of damaged boats if required
